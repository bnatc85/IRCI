💹 Valuation EV/EBITDA vs peers

What it is

Peer-relative “cheapness” based on EV/EBITDA (lower multiple ⇒ higher score). Percentiled vs the current peer set.

How we compute

  • Compute EV/EBITDA for each ticker (TTM EBITDA; fallback per your code if needed).
  • Compare to peer mean (ex-self); map to percentile (0–100).

What a HIGH Valuation score means
How to read
  • Your EV/EBITDA is lower than most peers (i.e., ‘cheaper’ on this metric).
  • Often reflects strong EBITDA vs EV, or market under-appreciation.
Actions to consider
  • Sustain EBITDA growth with clear KPI tracking (margin, unit economics).
  • Maintain capital allocation discipline (debt reduction increases score by lowering EV; buybacks can be neutral depending on cash impact).
  • Keep comps transparent—ensure peer set reflects your business mix.

Reminder: scores are peer-relative; improvements may move slowly if the whole peer set also improves.

💧 Liquidity Turnover • Amihud • Roll spread

What it is

How easily the stock trades: higher turnover and tighter spreads yield a higher score.

How we compute

  • Quarter medians: turnover, Amihud illiquidity (lower is better), Roll spread (lower is better).
  • Percentiles per metric; blended into a 0–100 dial.

What a HIGH Liquidity score means
How to read
  • Active trading, tighter spreads, and lower price impact.
  • Investors can enter/exit positions with minimal friction.
Actions to consider
  • Maintain predictable disclosure cadence to sustain attention.
  • Support market-making programs (where permitted) and robust IR access.
  • Keep index eligibility and free-float health on the radar.

Reminder: scores are peer-relative; improvements may move slowly if the whole peer set also improves.

🗂️ Coverage 8-K cadence • 10-Q/K timeliness

What it is

How promptly and consistently the company updates the market via SEC filings.

How we compute

  • Count of 8-Ks within quarter, and days from quarter-end to first 10-Q/K.
  • Percentile 8-K (higher=better) and timeliness (faster=better); blend.

What a HIGH Coverage score means
How to read
  • Timely 10-Q/10-K and regular 8-Ks for material updates.
  • The market is rarely in the dark between earnings cycles.
Actions to consider
  • Maintain filing discipline and IR calendar reliability.
  • Use 8-Ks for material milestones to keep expectations aligned.
  • Publish FAQs or investor letters to pre-empt confusion.

Reminder: scores are peer-relative; improvements may move slowly if the whole peer set also improves.

🧭 Trust (Sentiment/Calmness) Residual calm • Event calm • Media tone

What it is

Signals that investors “trust” the story: calm factor-adjusted trading and constructive news tone, especially around filings.

How we compute

  • Baseline calmness: std of CAPM/FF5 residuals (lower std ⇒ higher score).
  • Event calmness: residual shock within ±k days of 8-K/10-Q/K (smaller ⇒ higher score).
  • Media tone: FinBERT sentiment (VADER fallback) averaged in-quarter.

What a HIGH Trust score means
How to read
  • Calm factor-adjusted trading and constructive media tone.
  • Few surprises around filings; guidance credible.
Actions to consider
  • Keep guidance bands realistic; reinforce with KPI bridges.
  • Run consistent comms across IR/PR/legal; invest in Q&A quality.
  • Proactively engage press/analysts with data-rich materials.

Reminder: scores are peer-relative; improvements may move slowly if the whole peer set also improves.

Note: Percentile buckets are peer-relative. Improving a dial may require either operational changes (fundamentals) or communication/process improvements (disclosure, cadence, expectations).
