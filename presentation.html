The Solution

Four Dials. One Composite Score.

IRCI distills investor relations performance into four research-backed dimensions, each scored 0–100 against your peer group.

Valuation
35% Weight

How the market values your company relative to fundamentals. Good IR reduces information asymmetry, improving multiples.

35% Weight

How easily your stock trades without moving the price. Strong IR attracts institutional investors and tightens spreads.

Academically Grounded, Practically Powerful

Unlike generic AI estimates, IRCI's dollar attribution is built on peer-reviewed research by Bushee & Miller (2012), showing IR contributes 5–10% of firm value over time.

The formula spreads this across the typical 50-point gap between industry leaders and laggards, then scales by your company's enterprise value and model fit.

The result: a conservative, defensible number you can put in front of the CFO and the board.

Academic Foundation

Grounded in Peer-Reviewed Research

Every calculation, weight, and recommendation in IRCI is backed by published finance research.

Bushee & Miller (2012)

"The Role of the Business Press as an Information Intermediary"

Finding: IR programs contribute 5–10% to firm enterprise value through reduced information asymmetry.

Kirk & Vincent (2014)

"Professional Investor Relations within the Firm"

Finding: Companies with dedicated IR see lower bid-ask spreads, higher institutional ownership, and more analyst coverage.

Amihud (2002)

"Illiquidity and Stock Returns"

Finding: The Amihud ratio captures price impact per dollar traded—the gold standard for measuring market liquidity.

Fama & French (2015)

"A Five-Factor Model of Expected Stock Returns"

Finding: Five-factor residuals isolate company-specific volatility from market-wide effects—used in IRCI's Trust dial.
